# Cross-Dataset Comparison: FB Ads vs FB Posts vs Twitter Posts

## Overview

This document compares the three datasets analyzed in Task 3, all related to political
social media activity during the 2024 U.S. Presidential election. The datasets are:

| Dataset       | Rows    | Columns | Platform           |
|---------------|---------|---------|--------------------|
| FB Ads        | 246,745 | 41      | Facebook (paid ads) |
| FB Posts      | 19,009  | 56      | Facebook (organic posts) |
| Twitter Posts | 27,304  | 47      | Twitter/X (organic posts) |

---

## Shared Columns

All three datasets share **27 columns**, all of which are binary (0/1) message
classification indicators generated by the "illuminating" scoring system. These
fall into three categories:

**Message type indicators** (5 columns):
`advocacy_msg_type_illuminating`, `attack_msg_type_illuminating`,
`cta_msg_type_illuminating`, `image_msg_type_illuminating`,
`issue_msg_type_illuminating`

**Topic indicators** (14 columns):
`economy_topic_illuminating`, `health_topic_illuminating`,
`immigration_topic_illuminating`, `education_topic_illuminating`,
`environment_topic_illuminating`, `foreign_policy_topic_illuminating`,
`governance_topic_illuminating`, `lgbtq_issues_topic_illuminating`,
`military_topic_illuminating`, `race_and_ethnicity_topic_illuminating`,
`safety_topic_illuminating`, `social_and_cultural_topic_illuminating`,
`technology_and_privacy_topic_illuminating`, `womens_issue_topic_illuminating`

**Other classifiers** (8 columns):
`covid_topic_illuminating`, `engagement_cta_subtype_illuminating`,
`fundraising_cta_subtype_illuminating`, `voting_cta_subtype_illuminating`,
`incivility_illuminating`, `scam_illuminating`,
`freefair_illuminating`, `fraud_illuminating`

No engagement metrics, dates, or identifiers are shared across all three datasets.
Each platform uses its own naming conventions for these fields.

---

## Message Strategy Comparison

Comparing the mean values of message type indicators reveals significant differences
in how political messaging operates across platforms:

| Message Type      | FB Ads (mean) | FB Posts (mean) | Twitter (mean) |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Advocacy          | 0.549         | 0.549           | 0.564          |
| Attack            | 0.272         | 0.217           | 0.308          |
| Call-to-Action    | 0.573         | 0.133           | 0.110          |
| Image             | 0.223         | 0.149           | 0.226          |
| Issue             | 0.382         | 0.460           | 0.508          |

**Key observations:**

- **Advocacy messaging is consistent** across all three platforms at roughly 55%.
  This suggests that advocacy is a baseline strategy regardless of platform or
  whether the content is paid or organic.

- **Call-to-action dominates paid ads but not organic content.** CTA messaging
  appears in 57% of FB Ads but only 13% of FB Posts and 11% of Twitter posts.
  This makes sense: paid advertisements are designed to drive specific actions
  (donate, register, visit a website), while organic posts prioritize engagement
  and discussion.

- **Twitter is more attack-oriented.** Attack messaging is highest on Twitter
  (31%) compared to FB Ads (27%) and FB Posts (22%). The conversational nature
  of Twitter may encourage more confrontational messaging.

- **Issue-focused messaging is highest on organic platforms.** Twitter (51%) and
  FB Posts (46%) carry more issue-focused content than FB Ads (38%). Organic
  content appears more policy-substantive, while paid ads focus on mobilization.

## Topic Distribution Comparison

| Topic                | FB Ads (mean) | FB Posts (mean) | Twitter (mean) |
|----------------------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Economy              | 0.122         | 0.090           | 0.160          |
| Health               | 0.109         | 0.049           | 0.056          |
| Immigration          | 0.034         | 0.041           | 0.065          |
| Social & Cultural    | 0.106         | 0.062           | 0.052          |
| Women's Issues       | 0.081         | 0.026           | 0.023          |
| Incivility           | 0.188         | 0.128           | 0.179          |

**Key observations:**

- **Economy is the top topic on Twitter** (16%) but ranks lower on FB Posts (9%).
  Twitter's real-time conversational format may drive more economic policy debate.

- **Health is disproportionately present in FB Ads** (11%) compared to organic
  posts on either platform (5-6%). Health-related advertising likely reflects
  targeted campaign messaging around healthcare policy.

- **Women's issues appear primarily in FB Ads** (8.1%) versus organic posts
  (2.3-2.6%). This suggests that women's issues are more of a paid advertising
  strategy than an organic conversation topic.

- **Incivility is highest in FB Ads and Twitter** (~18-19%) and lower in
  FB Posts (13%). The lower incivility in FB Posts may reflect Facebook's
  content moderation or the nature of page-based posting versus ad-based
  messaging and Twitter's open format.

## Platform-Specific Columns

**FB Ads only:** `page_id`, `ad_id`, `estimated_spend`, `estimated_impressions`,
`estimated_audience_size`, `delivery_by_region`, `demographic_distribution`,
`bylines`, `currency`, `publisher_platforms`, `illuminating_mentions`

These columns reflect the paid advertising infrastructure: spending, audience
targeting, and delivery metrics that do not exist for organic content.

**FB Posts only:** `Facebook_Id`, `post_id`, `Likes`, `Comments`, `Shares`,
`Love`, `Wow`, `Haha`, `Sad`, `Angry`, `Care`, `Total Interactions`,
`Post Views`, `Total Views`, `Type`, `Overperforming Score`,
`Video Share Status`, `Video Length`, `Sponsor Id/Name/Category`

Facebook posts have granular reaction-type engagement metrics (love, wow, haha,
sad, angry, care) that provide richer sentiment signals than simple like counts.

**Twitter only:** `id`, `retweetCount`, `replyCount`, `likeCount`, `quoteCount`,
`viewCount`, `bookmarkCount`, `source`, `lang`, `isReply`, `isRetweet`,
`isQuote`, `month_year`

Twitter provides engagement metrics aligned with its platform mechanics (retweets,
quotes, bookmarks) and metadata about post type (reply, retweet, quote).

## Scale Differences

The three datasets differ substantially in size:

- **FB Ads** (246,745 rows) dwarfs the other two datasets, reflecting the sheer
  volume of paid advertising on Facebook during the election cycle.
- **Twitter Posts** (27,304 rows) represents a moderate volume of organic tweets.
- **FB Posts** (19,009 rows) is the smallest dataset, covering organic posts from
  a relatively small number of Facebook pages (21 unique pages).

Despite size differences, the shared illuminating indicators allow meaningful
cross-platform comparison of messaging strategies and topic emphasis.

---

## Conclusion

The cross-dataset analysis reveals that while advocacy messaging is a universal
strategy across platforms, the tactical approach differs significantly. Paid
Facebook ads emphasize call-to-action messaging and target specific policy
issues like health and women's rights. Organic posts on both Facebook and
Twitter are more issue-focused and less mobilization-oriented. Twitter shows
the highest rates of attack messaging and economic policy discussion,
consistent with its reputation as a more confrontational platform.

These patterns suggest that political campaigns use each platform strategically:
Facebook ads for mobilization and targeted policy messaging, Facebook posts for
broader engagement, and Twitter for policy debate and opposition attacks.
